Olive

Prioritzing Physical Health & Mental Wellness

What is Olive?

Olive is an application intended to allow health-conscious users to track their physical and mental health, overall well-being, and medical information. Provides resources such as articles, videos, and audio, that assist and teach users about their desired topics. Some of the most anticipated topics include blood pressure, weight, steps, etc.

My Role As A Designer

My main goal was to make Olive one of the most accessible applications users have interacted with This includes adding accessibility features for those who are vision and hearing impaired. Some of these features include subtitles, tap-to-speak, the ability to change font size, etc. It was also very important for me to use a comprehensible language, as you do not need a degree to use or understand the language used within Olive. Finally I wanted to make the interface aesthetically pleasing and calming. I felt as though this would allow users to further and consistently with Olive.

Olive Design Language

Olive is intended to provide users with a safe, healthy, and calming feel. This is emulated by “Olive Green” being the main color present in the high-fidelity design. The use of plain language to be understood by a wider audience. The IA used such as icons and language is commonly found on mainstream applications and platforms.

Olive Hypothesis

If both an accessible and user-flexible offline application is provided then we will achieve a 7% increase in user activity via sign up's, (guest) log-ins, and feature usage. This idea is centered on usage. Specifically onboarding as this and the potential features makes or breaks a user's desire to continue using an application or site. This can be validated by keeping track of how often specific buttons are clicked. For example, noting when the "subtitles" button is clicked

The Process High-Fi

The creation of Olive as a high-fidelity interactive prototype consisted of listening to and acknowledging feedback received from the various usability tests that were conducted. These revisions made the app more "real". Whereas the application did not look "real" or usable in its mid-fidelity stage. It now includes more well-known icons, language, and accessibility features. Despite Olive being high-fidelity many revisions are to come in regards to the aesthetic or look. I would like to include more images and less animated icons.

Self Reflection

My role as a designer working on Olive has been both informative and fulfilling. Olive started as a school project but has become so much more. I started with a vision and am bringing it to life day by day. To see the progress is incredible. From tailoring the experience and interactions with future users to creating an aesthetically pleasing color scheme. I have a better idea of user wants, desires, and expectations. This project has taught me a lot, and as a designer, I am still learning and improving!
